How to edit more than one tune :
--------------------------------

When your first tune was finished you
easily edit the second behind the end
byte ($ff or $fe) of the first one.
Do the same with the third tune.

Now you can switch between the songs
like explained in the menu before.

*The numbers in left-down-corner mean:

T1: trck-point  blck-pos  blck-count
T2: trck-point  blck-pos  blck-count
T3: trck-point  blck-pos  blck-count




    IMPORTANT:End the last Tune with

       $FE/$FF followed by $FD !




b) Track-Edit
   ----------

    -Number between $00-$2b jumps to
     block.
    -Number over $3f repeats the next
     block $xx-$3f times.   .g. 43 02
     repeats the block 02 four times.
    -Number over $7f transposes all the
     following tracks.
    -Number $ff starts the track over.
    -Number $fe stops playing.

3) Block-Edit
   ----------

* Notes   : e.g. c-3, d-6, g#3
* Sound   : snd.xx     xx = Sound Number
* Duration: dur.xx     xx = Note Length
* Glide   : gl:xx,y    y  = Delay before
                            glide
                       xx = Glide rate
                           (If over $7f,
                           then glide up
                           at rate xx-
                           $80)
